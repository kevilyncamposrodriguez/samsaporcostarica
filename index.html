WHO WE ARE

Samsa por Costa Rica, is a non-governmental organization, which emerged from the company Corporación Grupo SAMSA de Centroamerica S.A. We developed a Corporate Social Responsibility to mitigate environmental impact, protect wildlife and promote social development in conjunction with Rescate Wildlife Rescue Center


MISSION

Transmit to the people through education and consciousness, the importance of conserving and protecting the natural wealth of Costa Rica, in conjunction with Rescate Wildlife Rescue Center. Promoting the support of wild animals, inspired by the stories of the different species of rescued wild animals, in order to provide Rescate Wildlife Rescue Center the enought visibility and human support in its acctivities.


Vision

Become a leading organizationin supporting Wild Life Rescue Center inspired by real events, which promotes and encourages the integral well-being and protection of the different species of wild animals in the ecosystem.


Values

Responsibility: We assume the environmental and social commitment through our Samsa project for Costa Rica, for the different regions of the country.

Solidarity: We understand that working together is the main means to achieve social development, animal and environmental protection.

Teamwork: The contribution and skills of the people who participate is what allows us to achieve, as a unit, the objective in favor of animal, social and environmental welfare.

Equity: The goal sought is equal opportunities for all species in the different regions of the country.

The Samsa Por Costa Rica Project creates and trades digital assets (NFTs), inspired by vulnerable Costa Rican fauna. Participating and developing activities with other organizations, such as the Rescate Wildlife Rescue Center, which promotes wild-animal welfare.

Pita is a Puma Concolor, native from Costa Rica. The Puma Concolor is the second largest feline in size, not only in Costa Rica, but in America. The first place is taken by the jaguar. It is estimated that females can weigh approximately 44 to 66 pounds, while a male can weigh between 66 and 88 pounds.

Who is Pita? We learned about the stories of the animals that make up the Animal Rescate Wildlife Rescue Center, and we became aware of the puma Pita , which inspired us to start our first project. Pita spent two months with her mother and it was later kidnapped by hummans, she was made to live with humans and domestic cats as a pet, which generated a disorder of abilities and behaviors in the Puma concolor.

Pita Real Life at Rescate Wildlife Rescue Center

Pita was found walking very calmly around the house together with the domestic pets. It's totally the opposite, the expected behavior was shyness, fear or defense, so it is presumed that Pita had already been with that family for several years.

When this type of rescue occurs, it is called an extraction, and in addition to being completely unnecessary, it is also one of the main problems that Rescate Wildlife Rescue Center (Zooave) presents with the population.

Because of Pita entered to Rescate Wildlife Rescue Center (Zooave), being so small, it is very difficult for a feline that large and with such complex behavior to have the ability to develop all the necessary skills to survive in nature.

Felines, specifically pumas, require maternal care for between a year and a half to two years. Pita was only in contact with her mother for about only two months.

During 2021, 24% of the animals that the Rescate Wildlife Rescue Center (Zooave) received were unnecessary extractions, with animals that could have remained in their habitat with their parents, it would have guaranteed that they successfully reached adulthood and they would not have had to enter a Rescue Center, it is important that the population knows the importance of proper handling that wild animals should have when we see any animal within its own habitat.
